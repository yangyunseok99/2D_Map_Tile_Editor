Dreaming of becoming a game designer or developer? This tool is a magical utility that lets you create game map data by simply dragging your mouse, just like drawing in a paint tool, without any complex coding.

🎨 1. Drawing the Map (The Basics!)

Click on the desired tile (e.g., Wall, Monster, Bush) from the [Select Brush] panel on the left. Then, click and drag on the wide grid screen on the right. Colors will be painted continuously, just like filling Excel cells!

  • Eraser Tip: You don't even need to select the eraser from the palette. Just Right-Click and drag to enter eraser mode instantly!

🛠️ 2. Handy Magic Tools (Top Toolbar)

Look at the three tool buttons at the top center of the screen.

  • ✏️ Pencil: Use this to drag and paint only where you want.
  • 🪣 Paint Bucket: Click this to fill an empty room or a wide area with the same color at once. (No more tedious clicking!)
  • ↩️ Undo: Oops, made a mistake? Click this button or press Ctrl + Z on your keyboard. You can undo up to 20 times.

➕ 3. Creating Your Own Tiles (Blocks)

Need tiles like 'Lava' or 'Treasure Box' besides the default walls and monsters? Create them in the [Add New Tile] section at the bottom left.

  1. Click the color square to pick a color.
  2. Type a name like "Treasure Box" in the 'Tile Name' input.
  3. Click the [Add Tile] button, and it will magically appear in your palette above!
💡
Palette Backup Tip: It would be sad to lose your custom tiles when you reopen this site later, right? Click the Copy (Document Icon) button next to 'Select Brush' to copy your tile data. Save it in a notepad, and later click the Restore (Clipboard Icon) button to bring them back!

📏 4. Adjusting Map Size and View

  • Expand Map: Change the Cols and Rows numbers at the top right. (Up to 50x50)
  • View at a Glance: Move the [Zoom] slider left, located just above the map, to zoom out and see large maps easily.
  • Hide Lines: Uncheck [Show Grid] if you want to see how tiles seamlessly connect without grid lines.
  • Clear All: Click the red [Clear Map] button to start over from scratch.

💾 5. Save and Load Your Finished Map (Crucial!)

Once your map is complete, let's turn it into game data. Look at the bottom of the screen.

  • 📥 [Export JSON]: Click this to download your map as a level_data.json file. (Give this to your programmer!)
  • 📤 [Import (.json)]: Want to continue working on a map from yesterday? Select your downloaded file, and your map will reappear on the screen.
  • 🖼️ [Capture Image]: Click this when you need a picture of the map for your design documents! The map screen will be saved as a clean .png image.
Now, start clicking and dragging to create your own awesome dungeons and towns! 🎉
